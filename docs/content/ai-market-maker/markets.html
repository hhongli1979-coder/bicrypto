AI Market Maker Markets

Complete Market Configuration Guide

üéØ Overview

AI Market Maker Markets are the foundation of the market maker system. Each market connects to an existing ecosystem trading pair and configures how AI bots will trade on that pair, including target prices, price movement strategies, and liquidity settings.

üöÄ Accessing AI Markets

Navigate to the AI Market Maker Markets management:

Admin Panel ‚Üí Extensions ‚Üí AI Market Maker ‚Üí Markets

Or directly access via URL:

/admin/ai/market-maker/market

‚ûï Creating a New AI Market

1

Click "Create New Market"

Click the "+" button in the markets management interface to open the creation dialog.

2

Select Ecosystem Market

Choose an existing ecosystem market pair (e.g., BTC/USDT, ETH/USDT) from the dropdown.

üí° Note: Markets must be created in the Ecosystem extension first before they can be selected here.

3

Configure Price Settings

Set the target price and price movement strategy for this market.

4

Set Liquidity Parameters

Configure the real liquidity percentage and base/quote allocations.

5

Save and Activate

Save the market configuration. The market starts in INACTIVE status - activate it when ready to begin trading.

üìù Market Configuration Fields

Basic Information

Ecosystem Market
Select (Required)

The underlying ecosystem market pair this AI market will trade on (e.g., BTC/USDT).

Status
Select

Current operational status of the market:

ACTIVE INACTIVE PAUSED

Price Settings

Target Price
Decimal (Required)

The desired price that the AI bots will gradually move toward. Set this based on your market objectives.

Current Price
Decimal (Auto)

The current market price. This is updated automatically as trades execute.

Price Strategy
Select (Required)

How price should move toward the target:

GRADUAL_DRIFT Slow, steady movement
OSCILLATION Wave patterns around target
SUPPORT_RESISTANCE Bounce between levels
Spread Percentage
Decimal (Required)

The bid-ask spread maintained by market makers, expressed as a percentage (e.g., 0.5 = 0.5% spread).

Liquidity Settings

Real Liquidity Percent
Integer 0-100 (Required)

Percentage of orders that execute as real ecosystem orders vs AI-only simulated orders.

0% = All AI-only (no real trades) | 100% = All real orders

Base Balance
Decimal (Auto)

Current balance of the base currency (e.g., BTC) available for AI trading.

Quote Balance
Decimal (Auto)

Current balance of the quote currency (e.g., USDT) available for AI trading.

Daily Trade Limit
Integer (Required)

Maximum number of trades allowed per day for this market. Resets at midnight UTC.

Risk Management

Volatility Threshold
Decimal (Required)

Maximum allowed price volatility (as %). Trading pauses if exceeded. Default: 5%

Max Daily Loss
Decimal (Required)

Maximum loss allowed per day (as %). Trading stops if limit is hit. Default: 2%

üìä Price Movement Strategies

üìà

Gradual Drift

Price moves slowly and steadily toward the target over time. Best for organic-looking price discovery without sudden movements.

Use when: You want gradual price appreciation/depreciation that appears natural to traders.

„Ä∞Ô∏è

Oscillation

Price waves up and down around the target price in a sine-wave pattern. Creates trading opportunities and natural-looking volatility.

Use when: You want to maintain a price range while providing swing trading opportunities.

‚ÜîÔ∏è

Support & Resistance

Price bounces between defined support and resistance levels, mimicking technical analysis patterns traders expect.

Use when: You want price to behave according to technical analysis expectations.

üí° Best Practices

Starting a New Market

  • ‚Ä¢ Start Conservative - Begin with 0% real liquidity to test bot behavior
  • ‚Ä¢ Low Daily Limits - Set conservative daily trade limits initially
  • ‚Ä¢ Monitor Closely - Watch the first few hours of trading activity
  • ‚Ä¢ Gradual Increase - Slowly increase real liquidity % as you gain confidence

Target Price Management

  • ‚Ä¢ Realistic Targets - Set targets within reasonable range of current price
  • ‚Ä¢ Gradual Changes - Avoid sudden large target price changes
  • ‚Ä¢ Market Alignment - Consider external market conditions when setting targets
  • ‚Ä¢ Regular Review - Adjust targets based on market performance

Risk Management

  • ‚Ä¢ Set Loss Limits - Always configure max daily loss protection
  • ‚Ä¢ Volatility Buffers - Leave headroom in volatility thresholds
  • ‚Ä¢ Diversify Strategies - Use different strategies across markets
  • ‚Ä¢ Emergency Stop - Know how to quickly pause all markets if needed
